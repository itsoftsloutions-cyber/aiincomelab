## Wednesday — SEO for Absolute Beginners

### LinkedIn Post

**Headline: SEO for beginners — the 20% that drives 80% of results**

Most SEO advice is overcomplicated. Here's what actually matters when you're starting a blog:

**1. Keyword research (the right way)**
Don't target "AI" or "make money online" — those have 90+ difficulty scores.
Target "best AI tools for [specific niche]" or "how to [exact action] with AI."
Use ChatGPT as your keyword tool: "Give me 20 low-competition keywords in [niche] with search volume 100-1000."

**2. Write for featured snippets**
Google pulls the first clear answer it finds.
Structure your posts with the target question as an H2, then answer it in the first paragraph below.
Use lists, tables, and numbered steps. Google loves structure.

**3. Internal links = authority distribution**
Every post should link to 2-3 relevant older posts.
This passes "link juice" around your site and tells Google which pages are important.

**4. Consistency beats optimization**
One perfectly optimized post won't rank. 100 solid posts will.
Publish weekly. Fix small things as you go. Don't get stuck in SEO perfectionism.

**The best SEO strategy for beginners:**
Write 100 posts. Connect them with internal links. That's 80% of the work.
