Here is what I wish someone had told me about **write blog post with ai** when I started: it is simpler than the internet makes it seem, but harder than a five-step list suggests. The difference between success and frustration comes down to a few specific decisions.

## Minutes 1-5: keyword and intent research

Confirm search intent by scanning the top 3 Google results to understand what format and angle searchers expect.

Note questions from the People Also Ask section and related searches since these reveal what your post should cover.

Pick one primary keyword and 3 related supporting terms to incorporate naturally into subheadings and body text.

## Minutes 6-10: outline with AI

Prompt AI for a detailed 5-7 section outline including sub-questions and related topics discovered during research.

Add a custom section for original data or personal experience that AI cannot generate on its own.

Reorder sections putting the most valuable information first since readers often skim.

## Minutes 11-20: first draft

Work section by section prompting AI to write 200-250 words each while keeping the outline visible for topic adherence.

Do not edit as you go ? get the complete draft down first and fix imperfections in the dedicated editing pass.

Re-prompt with more specific instructions if AI goes off-topic rather than accepting low-quality output.

## Minutes 21-28: editing pass

Cut filler words, passive constructions, and repetitive phrases that make AI content feel bloated.

Insert at least 3 original observations or examples transforming the generic draft into content only you could write.

Verify every statistic and factual claim by cross-referencing trusted sources.

## Minutes 29-30: publish checklist

Write a title under 60 characters with the keyword near the front creating curiosity or promising a benefit.

Craft a meta description of 150-160 characters including the keyword and a compelling call to action.

Add internal links to 2 related posts, a featured image with alt text, and verify the URL slug matches the keyword.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I really write a quality post in 30 minutes using AI?**

Yes, once you practice the workflow. The timeline is realistic for 1,000-1,500 word posts. Longer posts may take 45-60 minutes but scale proportionally.

**Does this workflow work for any topic?**

Best for topics where you already have background knowledge. Add research time for unfamiliar subjects before the 30-minute clock starts.

**How do I ensure AI output sounds like me?**

The editing pass injects your voice and opinions. Also craft prompts specifying your tone ? conversational, professional, or authoritative ? to get closer to your natural voice from the draft stage.

## Final thoughts

I have used this workflow for hundreds of posts, and it consistently produces ranking content when editing is taken seriously. The most common mistake is skipping the editing pass to save minutes, turning a great post into generic filler. Follow the timing strictly.
