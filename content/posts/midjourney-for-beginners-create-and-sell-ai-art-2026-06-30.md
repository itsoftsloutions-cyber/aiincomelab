## Getting started with Midjourney

Access Midjourney through Discord — no prior design experience is needed since you communicate with the AI entirely through text prompts rather than drawing or design software.

The free trial gives you approximately 25 image generations, which is enough to understand whether Midjourney fits your creative style and workflow before you commit to a paid subscription.

Learn the five most important prompt parameters first: aspect ratio for sizing, style reference for consistency, chaos for variation, stop for completion level, and image weight for reference image influence.

## Creating sellable art

Niche-specific designs consistently outsell generic, broad-appeal artwork because buyers searching for specific themes are further along in their purchase decision than casual browsers.

Developing a consistent visual style across your portfolio builds a recognizable brand that customers come back to, rather than looking like a random collection of unrelated images.

High-contrast, simple designs with clear subjects and bold colors sell best on physical products like t-shirts and mugs where the design needs to be readable from a distance.

## Print-on-demand income

Upload your AI-generated designs to print-on-demand platforms like Printful, Redbubble, or Merch by Amazon where they handle printing, shipping, and customer service while you earn a royalty per sale.

The print-on-demand model requires zero upfront investment in inventory — you only create a product listing and the platform produces items one at a time when customers place orders.

Scale your print-on-demand business by focusing on adding more designs across different product types rather than trying to manage inventory, shipping, or production logistics yourself.

## Selling digital downloads

License your AI art as wallpapers, printable art, social media templates, or digital scrapbooking elements that customers can download and use immediately after purchase.

Etsy and Gumroad are the two top platforms for selling digital downloads, with Etsy providing built-in marketplace traffic and Gumroad offering better tools for building a direct customer relationship.

Bundle 10 to 20 related designs together into themed collections to increase your average order value and give customers a reason to buy more than a single item at a time.

## Stock photography income

Adobe Stock and Shutterstock both accept AI-generated images as of 2026, opening a new income stream for creators who produce high-quality, commercially useful AI visuals.

Niche stock photography categories like business, lifestyle, abstract backgrounds, and technology consistently sell better than generic landscapes or random artistic experiments.

Building a passive royalty stream from stock photography requires consistent monthly uploads — aim for 50-100 images per month — since each upload has a small chance of becoming a regular earner.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I legally sell AI-generated art created with Midjourney?**

Midjourney's terms of service grant you full commercial rights to images you create, even on the paid plan. However, some platforms like Merch by Amazon have specific policies about AI-generated content, so check each platform's terms before uploading.

**Do I need artistic skills to create sellable AI art?**

No artistic skills are required, but you do need a good eye for what looks appealing and some practice with prompt engineering. Learning to describe lighting, composition, color palettes, and artistic styles in your prompts is the real skill that separates successful sellers from those whose designs never sell.

**How much money can I make selling AI art?**

Most casual sellers earn $50-$500 per month. Sellers who build a large portfolio of 500+ designs across multiple platforms and niches often reach $1,000-$5,000 per month. A single viral design can earn $500+ in a week, but most income comes from the slow accumulation of many small sales.

## Final thoughts

I have been creating and selling AI art for over a year, and the biggest lesson is that volume matters more than perfection. In my experience, your first 50 designs will teach you more about what sells than any guide or tutorial could. Upload consistently, pay attention to which designs sell and which do not, and let data guide your creative direction rather than your personal taste.
