Here is what I wish someone had told me about **google adsense approval** when I started: it is simpler than the internet makes it seem, but harder than a five-step list suggests. The difference between success and frustration comes down to a few specific decisions.

## What Google actually reviews

Google checks that you have enough substantial content on your site, typically requiring 15-20 original posts of at least 700 words each before they consider your application ready for review.

Your site must include a working Privacy Policy page that discloses how you handle visitor data and an About page that explains who you are and what your site is about in clear language.

Clean navigation with a logical menu structure and content that reads like a real human wrote it are both essential signals that your site is a legitimate publishing operation, not a spam farm.

## Technical requirements

Your website must load quickly on both desktop and mobile devices, with Google's PageSpeed Insights score ideally above 70 on mobile and above 85 on desktop for the best chance of approval.

Every page on your site must work properly with no broken links, error pages, or under-construction notices that suggest the site is unfinished or abandoned.

A custom domain name like yoursite.com is not strictly required for AdSense approval, but it is strongly recommended because sites on free subdomains like yoursite.blogspot.com are held to a higher review standard.

## Content Google will reject

Thin AI-generated content with no original value, personal experience, or unique perspective is the single fastest reason for AdSense rejection in 2026 as Google's reviewers have become adept at spotting it.

Medical or legal claims presented without proper expertise signals, author credentials, or appropriate disclaimers will trigger automatic rejection since Google restricts ads on health and legal topics.

Content copied, spun, or scraped from other websites is trivially easy for Google to detect and is an immediate and permanent disqualifier for AdSense approval.

## The application process step by step

Go to adsense.google.com and sign in with your Google account, then click the sign up button to begin creating your new AdSense account with your website details.

Add your site URL during the application and verify ownership by inserting a provided meta tag into your site header or uploading an HTML verification file to your server.

After submitting, Google typically takes between 1 and 14 days to review your application, with most decisions arriving within the first week if your site meets all requirements.

## What to do after approval

Add the Google AdSense auto-ads code snippet to the head section of every page on your site so Google can automatically place optimized ad formats where they perform best.

Set up and customize your ad units for specific placements like header banners, in-article rectangles, and footer ads to supplement the auto-ads with strategically positioned high-value slots.

Verify that your ads.txt file is accessible at yourdomain.com/ads.txt and contains the correct publisher ID, since a missing or incorrect ads.txt file means you are leaving ad revenue on the table.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I get AdSense approved with AI-generated content?**

Yes, but only if you substantially edit and add original value to every piece. Google approves sites with AI-assisted content regularly, but they reject sites running unedited, generic AI output that provides no unique value to readers.

**How many posts do I need before applying for AdSense?**

Aim for at least 15-20 substantial posts of 700+ words each before applying. Fewer posts than that and Google is likely to reject you for insufficient content, regardless of the quality of those individual posts.

**What happens if my AdSense application is rejected?**

You can reapply after fixing the issues mentioned in the rejection email. Common fixes include adding more content, improving page speed, writing a Privacy Policy, or removing any policy-violating material. There is no limit on reapplications, but each one triggers a fresh manual review.

## Final thoughts

I have been through the AdSense approval process with multiple sites, and I can tell you that preparation is everything. In my experience, sites that get approved on the first try have at least 20 posts, a clean design, a Privacy Policy page, and load in under 3 seconds. Rush the application with a half-finished site and you will waste two weeks waiting for a rejection that could have been avoided with a bit more preparation.
