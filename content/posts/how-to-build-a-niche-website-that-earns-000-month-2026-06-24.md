Here is what I wish someone had told me about **niche website income** when I started: it is simpler than the internet makes it seem, but harder than a five-step list suggests. The difference between success and frustration comes down to a few specific decisions.

## Choose the right niche

Target niches with clear buyer intent and high ad rates like personal finance, software tools, or specialized health sub-niches.

Avoid niches dominated by major media brands like Forbes or WebMD since ranking against them requires resources you lack as a new site.

Personal finance, software reviews, and specific health sub-niches earn the highest ad rates for niche site operators.

## Content strategy for a niche site

Start with 20 informational posts answering common questions before ever asking visitors to buy through affiliate links.

Pivot to comparison articles and best-of roundups once you have an authority foundation, naturally incorporating affiliate recommendations.

Update every post ranking on page one or two every 6 months to protect rankings from newer competing content.

## SEO for a niche site

Long-tail keywords of 3-5 words are your competitive advantage since major brands focus on short, high-volume head terms.

Internal link from every informational post to money pages containing affiliate recommendations for passing authority.

Topical authority by covering every angle of your niche is the modern equivalent of PageRank and directly correlates with ranking strength.

## Monetization mix

Display ads from AdSense provide income from traffic alone, upgrade to Mediavine or AdThrive at 50,000 monthly sessions.

Join 3-5 affiliate programs paying 20-50% recurring commissions for products you genuinely recommend.

Sponsored posts from niche companies become available once your site reaches DA 30 and consistent traffic.
